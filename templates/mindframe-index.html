Why this exists

Popular psychology gets a lot of attention. Most of it is wrong.

Half of what shows up on social feeds under the banner of "psychology" was either never replicated, comes from a single small study, or is a confident generalization built on a foundation of almost nothing. The other half is fine, but framed for outrage rather than understanding.

MindFrame exists because there is a real, growing literature on how minds work - on attention, memory, sleep, habit formation, emotional regulation, and the wet biology underneath - and most of it never reaches the people who would benefit from it. Researchers write for other researchers. Journalists write for clicks. We try to write for the adult in between.

Every claim on this site is cited. Every effect size we mention is given context. When the evidence is mixed, we say so. When a popular idea has not held up to replication, we say that too.

Our editorial standards

  • We cite primary sources, not other blogs that cite the sources.
  • Effect sizes are reported in context. A statistically significant result is not the same as a meaningful one.
  • Pre-registration, sample size, and replication status are flagged where relevant.
  • If a topic is contested, the article shows the dissenting evidence.
  • No affiliate links inside research-driven articles.
  • Authors disclose conflicts of interest.
The Lab

Five short tests. Real instruments, faithfully implemented.

Each test runs entirely in your browser. Nothing is sent to a server, nothing is stored. Results appear inline and you can copy a sharable link with your result encoded in the URL.

Test 01 25 items · 4 min

Big Five (OCEAN) Personality Snapshot

A short version of the five-factor personality model: Openness, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, Neuroticism. Five questions per trait, likert response.

Test 02 12 items · 3 min

Adult Attachment Style

A short forced-choice quiz mapping responses onto the four-category attachment model (Secure, Anxious, Avoidant, Disorganized). Useful for context, not diagnosis.

Test 03 7 items · 3 min

Cognitive Reflection Test (CRT-7)

Seven trick questions that have an obvious wrong answer and a less obvious right one. Measures your tendency to override intuitive responses with deliberation.

Test 04 5 trials · 1 min

Visual Reaction Time

Press space or tap when the panel turns green. Five trials, slowest dropped. Compare your average to age-banded norms from the published reaction-time literature.

Test 05 Adaptive · 3 min

Digit Span - Working Memory

A sequence of digits flashes briefly. Type them back in order. The sequence grows by one each round until you miss twice in a row. Your highest correct length is your span.

These are educational self-assessments, not clinical diagnoses.

Tools

Three small utilities, built from the literature.

Lightweight tools you can use in under a minute. Each is grounded in a specific research tradition - implementation intentions, cognitive-behavioral reframing, and the spacing effect.

Habit Stack Generator

Pair a new behavior to an existing one using an implementation-intention prompt (Gollwitzer 1999), the single most reliable nudge in the behavior-change literature.

Cognitive Distortion Identifier

Type a thought, check the patterns that fit, get a CBT-style reframe template you can adapt. Based on Burns' original list of cognitive distortions.

Sleep-Memory Optimizer

Given when you studied and when you sleep, this gives the schedule for two review sessions that bracket sleep-dependent consolidation and use expanding intervals (spacing effect, Cepeda et al. 2008).
