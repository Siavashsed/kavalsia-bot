Daily Practice Tips

One small idea, every morning.

A short, useful note from my own practice. Read it in three minutes, try it for a week, and let the small things compound.

Today's tip

Practice slow enough to hear every note land.

Speed hides mistakes. When the tempo drops, your ear starts noticing the small things: a rushed sixteenth, a thumb that crosses too late, the moment a phrase wants to breathe. Fix those at half speed and full speed takes care of itself.

About Elyar

A musician who loves teaching almost as much.

I play, I compose, I teach. The same curiosity drives all three.

For me, music has always lived in the small details. The shift of a phrase, the breath before a downbeat, the moment a student finally hears what they were missing. Whether on stage with collaborators or one-on-one with a beginner, I'm chasing the same thing: clarity, fluency, and a kind of quiet joy that only practice can earn.

This site is where it all comes together. Free tips for anyone who wants to grow. Classes for those who want a real curriculum. Concerts for nights when music belongs on a stage. I'm glad you stopped by.
