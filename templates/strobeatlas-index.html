SAFFRON STONE on tempo, sleep, and the last hour
Recorded 12 May / London / 38 min
Q+A / DnB / CLOSING SLOTS

Saffron Stone

Ahead of her Boomtown set / Interview by Noor Halabi

Saffron Stone has closed eleven of the last fourteen nights she has played. She is the working DJ that other working DJs come down to watch. We caught her between a soundcheck and a Tube delay on a Tuesday afternoon in Hackney.

"The last hour is a different job. You stop choosing tracks. The room chooses them and you just queue them up fast enough."
Q / On tempo strategy
I never start above 168. People think DnB DJs walk in and detonate. I walk in and tune the room. The room tells you when to push. If you push first, you lose the room by 03:30 and you never get it back.
Q / On sleep
Two flights in a week and I am useless. I have a rule now. No second gig in the same 48 hours unless it is in walking distance. The set quality drops a clean fifteen percent without it. I can hear it in the recordings.
Q / On the Boomtown closing slot
I am bringing two USBs and a notebook. The notebook is for the order. The order is the whole job. I have been writing the last six tracks down on paper since 2019 and I will not change that for anybody.

The deck.

Six things we get asked at the door
FAQ / press / readers
01Are these paid features?+
No. We pay our own door and our own drinks. If we ever cover something where money changed hands, we will say so at the top in mono caps. Promise.
02Can I pitch a night?+
Yes. Drop the line up, the date, the cap, the city and one sentence on why it matters. Pitches go to the newsletter reply address. We read all of them, we cover what we can.
03Do you publish photos of the crowd?+
Rarely, and never without consent. Most of our gallery shots are crowd silhouettes, light, room geometry, or the dancers who actively asked to be shot. The dancefloor stays the dancefloor.
04How do you choose cities?+
We follow the rooms that take risks. A new venue, a strange line up, a residency that refuses to be safe. Big festivals already have enough cameras pointed at them.
05What is in the newsletter?+
A weekly guest list. Five to seven nights we think are worth your time, with the door price, the closing time, and a one line read on the vibe. Sent every Thursday at 4pm UTC.
06Who runs Strobe Atlas?+
Independent. Founded by Riv Solano in 2024. Self funded by reader subscriptions and a small ring of cultural sponsors who have agreed never to influence the writing.
