Acquisition Notes

Two short essays
Note 014.A / On Buying Slowly

The case for living with one work for a full year before adding another.

A small collection grows in the way a garden grows, which is to say, in spite of intention. The first acquisition is always the easiest. It is the second that decides everything.

I have been recommending the same exercise to private collectors for nearly a decade now. Buy one piece. Live with it for twelve months. Move it three times. Then, and only then, consider what it asks for as a companion.

Imogen Salinger / 6 min
Note 014.B / On Provenance

Why the second owner of a painting matters more than the first.

The first owner of a work is usually a friend, a patron, or the artist's mother. They tell us very little. The second owner is the one who chose, and choice is the only honest record we keep.

I keep a small notebook of second-owner stories. They are, almost without exception, more interesting than the studio anecdotes that surround a work's making.

Imogen Salinger / 5 min

From the Editor

A short letter, in answer to the usual questions.

These are the five questions readers send most often. I have tried to answer them in the spirit they were asked: briefly, without affectation, and with the door left open.

Q.How often does Folio Atelier publish?+
Two issues a season, four seasons a year. Between issues, the Index receives one or two new entries each fortnight. We do not publish on holidays, weekends, or when the writer feels rushed.
Q.Is the printed catalogue available to readers?+
Yes. Each issue is set in lead by a small letterpress in Antwerp and bound by hand in an edition of 600. Subscribers receive the printed catalogue by post; the online edition is free, in keeping with our reading-room philosophy.
Q.How do you choose which ateliers to visit?+
Slowly, and almost always on recommendation from other artists we have already visited. We do not accept pitches from galleries or representatives. We do read every letter sent by a working artist.
Q.Do you sell or broker work featured in the index?+
No. Folio Atelier is editorial, not transactional. When readers ask after a particular work, we forward the message to the artist, with their permission, and step out of the conversation.
Q.May I write or photograph for the publication?+
We commission a small roster each season. If your practice is in long-form criticism, studio reportage, or quiet documentary photography, please write to the editor with two recent pieces and a paragraph about a room you have not been able to forget.
