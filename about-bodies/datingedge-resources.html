Quick Toolkit

Checklists and starters to keep close

Reference these before a first date or whenever the nerves show up.

Conversation Starters

Skip the small talk that goes nowhere. Open with questions that invite a real answer.

  • "What is something you changed your mind about recently?"
  • "What does an ideal slow Sunday look like for you?"
  • "What are you a little obsessed with right now?"

Red-Flags Checklist

Patterns worth pausing on - not to judge fast, but to stay clear-eyed early.

  • Speaks about every ex as "crazy" or all to blame.
  • Pushes past a no, then frames you as the problem.
  • Love-bombs fast, then goes cold without warning.

Green-Flags Checklist

The quieter signs of a good match - the things that matter more than chemistry alone.

  • Curious about you without making it a quiz.
  • Handles a small letdown with grace, not sulking.
  • Their actions and their words tend to match.

First-Date Prep

A short routine to settle your nerves before you head out the door.

  • Pick a public, easy-to-leave spot you both know.
  • Set a soft time limit so there is no pressure.
  • Aim to be curious, not impressive.

Attachment Style Primer

A plain-language map of secure, anxious, and avoidant patterns - and how they meet.

  • Spot your default response to closeness and distance.
  • Learn which pairings need extra communication.
  • Styles can shift - none of them are a sentence.

Texting Without Spirals

Simple rules to keep early texting calm instead of all-consuming.

  • Match effort roughly - do not chase or out-wait.
  • Move toward a real plan within a few days.
  • A slow reply is data, not a personal verdict.
Recommended Reading

Books we genuinely send people to

No affiliate noise - just titles that hold up against the research.

Attached

Amir Levine & Rachel Heller

The clearest mainstream introduction to attachment theory. If you have ever felt anxious or shut down in dating, this explains why - without shame.

Hold Me Tight

Dr. Sue Johnson

Written for couples, but useful far earlier. It reframes conflict as a bid for connection rather than a sign something is broken.

How to Not Die Alone

Logan Ury

A practical, kind look at the habits that quietly sabotage dating, from a behavioral scientist who has coached thousands.

The Course of Love

Alain de Botton

A novel that doubles as relationship education - honest about what long-term love really asks of two ordinary people.
