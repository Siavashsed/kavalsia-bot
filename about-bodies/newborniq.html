About NewbornIQ

Calm, reliable guidance for the first year of parenthood.

NewbornIQ exists for the 2 a.m. moments - when the questions feel urgent, the advice online feels contradictory, and you just need someone trustworthy to tell you what is normal. We are here for exactly that.

Why We Exist

New parenthood is hard enough without the noise.

Every new parent meets the same wall of information. One source insists on a strict schedule, the next calls it harmful. A forum thread sparks a worry that a pediatric study would quietly settle in a sentence. The result is not knowledge - it is overwhelm, layered on top of exhaustion.

NewbornIQ was built to be the calm voice in that storm. Every guide is grounded in pediatric research and current AAP guidance, then written in plain, gentle language by parents who have lived the same sleepless weeks you are living now.

We do not take sponsored advice, and we do not push a single "right" way to parent. Fed is best. A bed-sharing guide and a crib guide sit side by side, because your family, your baby, and your circumstances are the ones that matter. Our job is to give you accurate information and the confidence to make your own call.

What We Stand For

The standards behind every guide

These four commitments decide what we publish - and how we write it.

Evidence-Based

Every guide references pediatric research and AAP guidelines, not anecdote or trend. When the science is unsettled, we say so.

Parent-Written

Real experience from parents who have been exactly where you are - so the guidance is practical, not just technically correct.

Always Updated

Content is reviewed and refreshed as pediatric guidance evolves, so what you read reflects current best practice.

No Judgment

We present options without shame. You know your baby best - we simply make sure you have the full picture.

Our Approach

Guidance that meets you week by week

Rather than one overwhelming manual, NewbornIQ is structured to give you exactly what is relevant right now.

Step One

What is normal this week

We start by reassuring. Most of what worries new parents is well within the healthy range, and naming that range calms the panic.

Step Two

What to actually do

Clear, specific steps you can take today - not vague encouragement. Every guide ends with something concrete.

Step Three

What comes next

A gentle preview of the milestone or change ahead, so transitions feel expected instead of alarming.

Step Four

When to call your doctor

And just as importantly, the clear signs that mean it is time to stop reading and seek real medical care.
