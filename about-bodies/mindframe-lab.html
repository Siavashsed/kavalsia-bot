Test 01
Big Five Personality
25 items · approx. 6 minutes · TIPI-based short form
You will get Personality profile Radar chart Shareable link

The five-factor model is the most replicated structure in personality psychology. This 25-item short form measures each factor with five questions, reverse-scored where applicable, and renders a radar chart of your profile.

Openness to experience, Conscientiousness, Extraversion, Agreeableness, and Neuroticism. These five factors account for roughly half of the variance in personality across cultures and age groups.

Test 02
Attachment Style
12 items · approx. 5 minutes · Bartholomew-Horowitz four-category model (1991)
You will get Attachment type Style description Distribution table

Adult attachment theory proposes that early caregiving experiences shape templates for close relationships. This 12-item forced-choice quiz maps your responses onto the four-category model developed by Bartholomew and Horowitz (1991).

Attachment style is not a fixed trait. It varies across relationships and can shift over time, particularly with therapy or consistent secure relationships.

Test 03
Cognitive Reflection Test (CRT-7)
7 items · approx. 5 minutes · Frederick (2005), extended version
You will get Reflection score Per-question breakdown Percentile

The Cognitive Reflection Test measures the tendency to override a salient intuitive response with a deliberative, correct one. The original three-item version (Frederick, 2005) has been extended to seven items here.

Higher CRT scores predict resistance to cognitive biases in independent studies. They are not a measure of intelligence, though moderate correlations with working memory exist.

Test 04
Visual Reaction Time
5 trials · approx. 3 minutes · variable foreperiod design
You will get Avg response time Percentile vs norms Trial breakdown

Visual reaction time measures the latency between perceiving a stimulus and producing a response. This task uses a variable foreperiod design to reduce anticipatory responses.

Typical reaction time in adults: 200-300 ms. Screen and touch latency adds 30-50 ms on mobile. Compare yourself across sessions, not across devices.

Test 05
Digit Span (Working Memory)
Adaptive · approx. 5 minutes · WAIS-IV subtest paradigm
You will get Digit span length WAIS-IV comparison Adaptive score

The digit span subtest measures the phonological loop component of working memory. An adaptive procedure finds your maximum forward span, the longest sequence you can reliably reproduce.

Miller (1956) proposed 7 plus or minus 2 as the typical adult capacity. More recent work suggests 4 plus or minus 1 as the true capacity before chunking is factored out.
