The Concept

Two separate conversations. One shared understanding.

Most couples therapy has one AI, one therapist, one shared space. Confer is different. Privacy allows honesty. Honesty produces better data. Better data produces a more useful report.

Private by design

Your AI never communicates with your partner's AI until you both consent explicitly on day 15. Your sessions are yours alone.

Evidence-grounded

The program structure draws on attachment theory, cognitive-behavioral principles, and the Gottman method. Not advice. Structured reflection.

A program, not a subscription

15 days. Then a joint report. Then an optional second season on a specific theme. You do not sign up for forever.

The Program

Fifteen days. Two private streams. One joint picture.

The Confer Report

Day 15: both streams converge.

The two AI therapists -- having worked with each partner separately -- compare notes, identify patterns, contradictions, and areas of potential growth, and produce a single joint report delivered to you both simultaneously.

Relationship strengths

Areas where both partners independently reported feeling seen, safe, or aligned

Conflict patterns

Recurring dynamics identified from both perspectives, with the other side's view attached

Communication gaps

Topics one partner raised frequently that the other never mentioned

Attachment dynamics

How each partner's style interacts with the other's in specific situations

3 exercises

Specific, research-backed activities to work on together in the next 30 days

Season 2 options

If you want to go deeper: themed arcs on intimacy, co-parenting, or communication
